Survey Shines a Light on the Medford AfterSchool Crisis

The following data and testimonials were collected via an informal survey conducted by @parentsworkingforbettermps@gmail.com in order to confirm that the lack of adequate afterschool programming in our town is in fact a crisis for working families.


How can we call this a crisis?

Medford continues to grow and after-school care far lags demand; here are the numbers to prove it:


  • In 2022 there are fewer students in MPS after-school care than in 2018, 2019, or 2020
  • While enrollment has declined, demand has increased substantially;
    • In 2019 there were 50 families on the waitlist;
    • today there are 144 families on the waitlist
  • Over 78% of new applicants did not get into the program!


    (all values from public records received March 24, 2022 and March 12, 2021)


    Who responded to the survey?
    Survey Start Date: March 19th; As of May 16th we have 192 responses


    		•

  • Of the 125 families with a child enrolled at an MPS school
  • only 21% (26) are in the MPS based after-school program; of those….
    • 42% (11) got in the program because of an older sibling already enrolled
    • 42% (11) were enrolled through the lottery
    • Three families were enrolled off the waitlist (one after four years waiting…)
    • One family was enrolled five years ago before there was a lottery / waitlist


  • 53% (66) of families are on the waitlist via the lottery


  • At least 11% of families were not aware of the lottery and had to make their own arrangements


  • And a few other families either chose not to sign up for the lottery because word of mouth provided insight that it was unlikely to fall in the family’s favor or they already had support, or worse because they are afraid there is no support for their child with special needs


    Respondents to the survey cover all of MPS, including families in the following elementary districts: Brooks, Roberts, McGlynn, and Missituck


    Additional community interest in this topic:
  • Over 11% of respondents have school-aged children enrolled in private school; Most commented a core driver choosing private over MPS was lack of after school care
  • Over 26% of respondents have children in preschool / pre-K and are already very concerned about the lack of after-school care; word is spreading in the community that this is a crisis and families are already feeling tremendous stress - or even regretting the move to Medford, MA


Response to Solving After-School Care on Our Own:


Of the 99 families at MPS who are not in the MPS after-school program:


97% would participate in the after-school program (at least part-time) if more space was available


Of the families with preschool / Pre-K aged children: 100% desire to have a spot in the MPS afterschool program at kindergarten enrollment

No one wants to solve a systemic city crisis on our own… why not?
